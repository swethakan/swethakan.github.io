Stakeholder

Los Angeles Times

At the start of the pandemic, the Los Angeles Times needed a central location to host and distribute all data we acquired about the state of the virus in California.

Problem Space

Find, visualize, and distribute data

We worked with over 50 county agencies in California to gather comprehensive data about the state. We had to design our dashboard, visuals, and more to make it easy for regular users to understand what the data means.

Solution

Series of dashboards

We seperated the information into multiple dashboards specifically for people concerned about the state’s unemployment statistics, the state of reopenings, racial inequity, etc. Our main dashboard was one that tracked infections.

Understanding the problem Space

We filtered information given to is by the California Department of Public Health. (Swetha Kannan / Los Angeles Times)

We had to get the initial page up within 3 days of starting our work on the tracker. California had started whispers of possibly going into lockdown and as it became a very real possibility, we rushed to mockup the dashboard in Adobe XD, analyze how competitors like the New York Times are visualizing tsimilar data, talk to the California Department of Public Health to gather data, and publish the dashboard.

Initially, we published with only the state’s coronavirus data but as we continued to talk to county officials, we found that there was a huge gap between the information that the county has and the outdated information of the state. Because fo this, we made the decision to start using county data for more accurate information.

After publishing the initial dashboard, we had a rush of reader feedback asking for this or that feature. It would have been too much to implement into a single page and therefore we began seperating out the dashboard into ultiple different pages.

Goals

After we published in March 2020, we contributed various improvements and expansions for the duration of the pandemic.

We wanted to provide California residents with an informative, convenient, and honest look at the State’s coronavirus data for the duration of the pandemic.

The process

Hi-fi mockup created used Abode XD (Swetha Kannan / Los Angeles Times)

Bubble map styling using MapBox. (Swetha Kannan / Los Angeles Times)

To create the graphics on the page, we used a variety of javascript libraries -- most notably D3. The interactive city map on the page was made using Nearmap and the Nearmap API. Beyond the visuals, the page also uses python scrapers that grabs information from a google sheet where we tracked the growth of the virus across California. This scraper was able to easily update our page through just a few terminal commands.

To initially design the page, we used Adobe XD to mockup our ideas.

This page was updating at a minimum about 4 times a day from publishing in March until now. To do this, we set up an extensive backend to the webpage including scrapers that helped us scrape government websites for the latest numbers on the virus, manual scheduled to review these sites daily, and also an unprecedented effort to combine this tracking effort with newsrooms across the state including the San Francisco Chronicle.

My major contributions were the topper, summary box, city and county map, maintenance of the data, and generally helping others in the production and upkeep of the page.

The results

The story published in March of 2020. It is still being updated.

This page was hugely popular among both subscribers and non-subscribers alike. For the first few months of the pandemic, it had over thousands of people on the page at any single time, pulling in record-breaking numbers of pageviews and subscribers for the Times. For all the months of the pandemic, the page consistently proved popular with our subscribers.
